# Virton Equity Negotiation Baseline

This page captures CK's equity negotiation baseline for Virton, based on the clarified role history: early core employee who grew into a de facto CTO role, not a legal/cofounder CTO position.

## Situation definition

- **Joined from the company start**, but as **technical team lead / employee position**.
- **Initial role**: led existing maintenance and technical-support work.
- **Current role**: Virton new-platform PM + development + infrastructure + de facto CTO responsibilities.
- **Official title**: not formally CTO yet.
- **Current equity**: 0%.

This is best framed as **early core employee → de facto CTO for the new platform**, not as a formal cofounder case.

## Negotiation baseline

The working equity range is **5–15% total economic upside**, with structure depending on how much is immediate stock versus options.

| Scenario | Common stock | Stock options | Note |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Floor | 3% | 5% | Below this, the grant risks becoming symbolic rather than meaningful. |
| Realistic target | 5–7% | 8–10% | Main negotiation baseline. |
| Ideal strong ask | 10% | 15% | Aggressive position if leverage is high. |

Recommended anchor:

- **5% common stock + 5% stock options = 10% total**, or
- **10–12% stock options only** if the company resists immediate stock.

## Why this is not a cofounder claim

Do not frame the ask as "cofounder equity." The stronger and more defensible framing is **core early employee plus current de facto CTO / product founder for the Virton line**.

Reasons:

1. CK did not share the original founding capital risk as a cofounder.
2. The company began with existing maintenance/support work, while Virton is the newer product line where CK became central.
3. CK was not externally positioned as official CTO from the beginning.

This avoids triggering resistance while still distinguishing CK from a late-stage hired CTO.

## Strong negotiation cards

### Virton is effectively CK's product contribution

- A substantial part of company revenue is tied to Virton.
- Zero-churn operational stability is a technical contribution CK can claim.
- CK is closer to the **product-line founder** of Virton than to a replaceable employee on an existing line.

### Replacement risk

- Proxmox + Ceph + GPU infrastructure full-stack expertise is scarce in Korea.
- If CK leaves, Virton operation and roadmap continuity become materially risky.

### Five-year tenure

- Loyalty and accumulated contribution have already been proven.
- A grant can be framed as retention of the safest high-leverage person, not just a reward.

### Future roadmap ownership

- GS certification, new features, HA cluster direction, and infrastructure roadmap depend heavily on CK.

## Negotiation cautions

- Avoid the term **cofounder**; use "early core member + current CTO-role responsibilities" instead.
- If the company proposes around 5%, improve structure rather than only arguing percentage: add options, retroactive vesting, or better leaver terms.
- If the company proposes 1–2%, treat it as a symbolic recognition grant rather than real CTO-level compensation.
- Push for vesting recognition because five years have already elapsed: for example, **50% immediate vesting + remaining over two years**, or already-vested recognition plus a future grant.

## Non-percentage terms to protect

1. Retroactive vesting recognition for five years of service.
2. Tag-along rights.
3. Some dilution protection.
4. Financial information access rights.
5. Fair good-leaver / bad-leaver treatment.
6. Written agreement; no oral promise.
