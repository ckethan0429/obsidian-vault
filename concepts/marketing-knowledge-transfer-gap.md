# Marketing Knowledge Transfer Gap

This page captures a recurring founder belief: practical marketing methods often emerge in one market, then diffuse into other markets years later through books, courses, agencies, and local influencers.

## Thesis from the source
- US and Japan are described as being 5-10+ years ahead of Korea in some areas of marketing practice
- Funnel concepts are presented as longstanding ideas abroad that reached mainstream visibility in Korea much later
- Studying first-generation overseas practitioners may be more useful than buying expensive local derivative courses

## Implications for startup operators
- Prefer source material over derivative summaries when possible
- Map ideas by origin, not by current social-media popularity
- Distinguish between original frameworks, local translation, and local repackaging
- Use local operators for market adaptation, but not necessarily for first exposure to the idea

## Open questions
- Which specific frameworks truly arrived later in Korea versus merely becoming mainstream later?
- Which overseas experts have the strongest evidence of transferable results?
- Which ideas fail when imported without cultural or channel adaptation?
