# Site Observation Archive

A site observation archive is a personal knowledge workflow for capturing the sites CK actually sees, then turning those visits into reusable wiki knowledge instead of one-off browsing history.

This is especially useful for high-signal platforms like X/Twitter, Threads, ChatGPT, Claude, Instagram, and YouTube, where the important thing is often not the page itself but the pattern behind the visit: what was being researched, what was repeated, what decision was being made, and what should be remembered later.

## Core idea

- **Raw capture first** — preserve the original page, thread, or session context in `raw/`
- **Structured filing second** — promote durable takeaways into `entities/`, `concepts/`, `comparisons/`, or `queries/`
- **No blind summarization** — keep enough context to reconstruct why the visit mattered
- **Repeatable workflow** — the same pattern should work across social feeds, chat apps, and video platforms

## Practical capture rules

1. Use browser/CDP when the site is login-dependent or session-specific.
2. Save a raw transcript or snapshot for the visit.
3. Create a wiki page only when the site or topic is recurring, strategically important, or central to a broader pattern.
4. If the page is about a platform, link it to broader workflows like [[korean-digital-service-automation]] and [[virton-career-and-business-profile]].
5. If the page is about a technical capture workflow, link it to [[ck-technology-engineering-profile]].

## What should be stored

- Repeated research topics
- Important threads or posts that changed thinking
- Login-gated pages that cannot be reconstructed later
- Product research, competitor research, and content ideas
- Signals about preferences, habits, or recurring questions

## What should not be stored as its own page

- Random low-signal browsing
- One-off clicks with no reusable meaning
- Pages that are only interesting for a few minutes
