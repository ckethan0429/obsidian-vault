# Korean Public Support Programs

A structured view of public-sector startup support surfaces in Korea, especially where founders can discover grants, policy support, R&D opportunities, employment incentives, and SME support programs.

## Core discovery surfaces from the source
- K-Startup
- 기업마당
- 소상공인24
- 소상공인진흥공단
- 고용24 / 고용복지센터
- 범부처통합연구지원시스템
- SMTECH
- 정보통신산업진흥원
- NIA
- 한국산업기술기획평가원(KEIT)

## Why this matters
For early-stage founders, the hard part is often not just applying but knowing which system is the canonical intake point. These services appear to function as intake, notice aggregation, eligibility discovery, or grant management layers.

## Useful subcategories
- Business commercialization and startup funding
- SME support and operator education
- Employment and hiring support
- R&D funding and technical program management
- Digital/AI and ICT-focused support programs

## Open questions
- Which of these portals are best for earliest-stage pre-revenue startups?
- Which are mainly notice boards versus true application systems?
- Which require separate regional or sectoral follow-up?
