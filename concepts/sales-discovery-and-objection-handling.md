# Sales Discovery and Objection Handling

This page focuses on the conversational mechanics of B2B selling: asking better questions, uncovering real pain, and handling objections as part of the buying process rather than as terminal rejection.

## Discovery principles from the source
- Use open-ended questions to get detailed context
- Prefer "what" and "how" over defensive "why" questions
- Use narrowing follow-up questions to reach the real need behind the surface request
- Use mirroring to encourage elaboration and rapport

## Objection handling principles
- Treat objections as signals of missing confidence, not necessarily rejection
- Use the 333 rule to review calls and build a learning loop
- Apply 3F (Feel, Felt, Found) to validate concern and show precedent
- Use boomerang reframing carefully when an objection can be reframed as a strength
- Use labeling to acknowledge emotion and lower tension

## Why it matters
For founder-led sales and solution sales, discovery quality usually determines everything downstream: proposal accuracy, pricing confidence, negotiation leverage, and close probability.
