# Proposal and Closing Strategy

This page captures the part of B2B sales where value articulation, negotiation framing, and trust-building converge.

## Negotiation principles from the source
- Avoid shallow midpoint compromises when they obscure the real need
- Use loss aversion by clarifying the cost of inaction or delay
- Aim for genuine buyer recognition ("that's right") rather than forced agreement

## Proposal-writing principles
- Use numbers and ROI rather than vague claims
- Speak in the customer's business language, not product vanity language
- Provide a concrete roadmap with action steps and timing

## Closing and trust
- Closing is treated as part of service delivery, not just pressure at the end of the funnel
- Positive posture, confidence in mutual agreement, and disciplined response patterns matter
- Trust is reinforced through personalization, transparency, proof, and steady communication

## Sales infrastructure
The source highlights CRM and call/collaboration tooling as key infrastructure for repeatable revenue operations.
